Zambia Reaches Paris Club Agreement on $6.3 Billion Debt Restructuring

Zambia — 20-01-2025 — Reuters
Zambia has reached a landmark agreement with the Paris Club on restructuring $6.3 billion of its external debt, marking a significant step forward in the country's debt sustainability efforts. The agreement includes a 40% NPV reduction and extends maturities by 20 years, with the first principal payment deferred until 2028. This follows Zambia's 2020 default and represents the first major breakthrough under the G20 Common Framework. The deal paves the way for similar restructurings in other heavily indebted African nations.

IMF Board Approves $3 Billion Extended Credit Facility for Ghana

Ghana — 19-01-2025 — IMF
The IMF Executive Board has approved a 36-month Extended Credit Facility arrangement for Ghana worth approximately $3 billion (SDR 2.242 billion). The program aims to restore macroeconomic stability and debt sustainability while protecting the vulnerable. Ghana's debt-to-GDP ratio is projected to decline from 88.1% in 2022 to 55% by 2028, supported by fiscal consolidation measures and structural reforms. The first disbursement of $600 million is expected immediately.

Argentina's Debt Sustainability Analysis Shows Improved Metrics

Argentina — 18-01-2025 — Bloomberg
Argentina's latest debt sustainability analysis reveals improved metrics as inflation continues to decline and fiscal consolidation measures take effect. The debt-to-GDP ratio has fallen to 78.5% from 89.2% in 2023, while the primary balance improved to -1.2% of GDP. The analysis projects debt sustainability under the current IMF program, with the ratio expected to decline to 65% by 2027. Market confidence has improved, reflected in narrowing sovereign spreads.

World Bank Introduces New Climate-Resilient Debt Instruments

Global — 17-01-2025 — World Bank
The World Bank has launched a new suite of climate-resilient debt instruments (CRDIs) designed to help vulnerable countries manage climate-related financial risks. The instruments include pause clauses that automatically suspend debt service payments following climate disasters, and state-contingent debt instruments linked to climate resilience metrics. Initial pilot programs will be implemented in the Caribbean and Pacific regions, with potential for broader adoption across developing nations.

Sri Lanka Completes Bondholder Vote on $12.5 Billion Restructuring

Sri Lanka — 16-01-2025 — Financial Times
Sri Lanka has successfully completed its bondholder vote on the restructuring of $12.5 billion in international sovereign bonds, with 85% of creditors approving the deal. The restructuring includes a 30% haircut and extends maturities by 10 years, with the first principal payment due in 2028. The agreement follows Sri Lanka's 2022 default and represents a key milestone in the country's economic recovery program supported by the IMF.
Score: 92/100

Ethiopia and Chad Submit Common Framework Applications

Ethiopia, Chad — 15-01-2025 — Reuters
Ethiopia and Chad have formally submitted applications to the G20 Common Framework for debt treatments, joining Zambia and Ghana in seeking coordinated debt relief. Ethiopia's external debt stands at approximately $28 billion, while Chad's is around $3 billion. Both countries face significant debt sustainability challenges and are seeking comprehensive debt restructuring under the framework. The applications will be reviewed by the Paris Club and other official creditors.
