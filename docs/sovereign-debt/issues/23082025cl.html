Debt sustainability concerns mounted across multiple jurisdictions with debt-to-GDP ratios exceeding pre-pandemic levels.

Forward Trends

1. Japan 30-Year Government Bond Yield Hits Record High of 3.10%

Date: 15-08-2025 | Country: Japan | Categories: Debt Sustainability, Market Developments
Japanese government bond markets experienced historic turbulence as 30-year yields surged to an unprecedented 3.10% on 15 August 2025, marking the highest level on record and eclipsing previous peaks from the late 1990s. The yield spike coincided with the Bank of Japan's decision to raise policy rates from 0.25% to 0.5%, representing the highest benchmark rate since 2008. Finance Ministry projections indicate provisional interest rates for FY2026 government bonds at 2.6%, the highest in 17 years, creating substantial fiscal pressure given Japan's debt-to-GDP ratio approaching 250%. The dramatic yield adjustment triggered concerns about potential carry trade unwinding similar to the August 2024 crisis, with implications extending beyond domestic markets to global sovereign debt pricing mechanisms.

2. ICE Rejects Adding EU Joint Debt to Sovereign Bond Indexes

Date: 19-08-2025 | Country: European Union | Categories: Market Access, Structural Reforms
Intercontinental Exchange Inc. delivered a significant setback to European Union financing strategies by rejecting proposals to include EU joint debt instruments in sovereign bond indexes on 19 August 2025. ICE cited 'lack of consensus on how best to classify the issuer' as the primary rationale for maintaining EU bonds' supranational entity status rather than sovereign classification. This decision perpetuates higher borrowing costs for EU joint debt compared to similarly-rated European government bonds, undermining the bloc's efforts to establish NextGenerationEU and other joint instruments as sovereign equivalents. The rejection affects approximately €800 billion in outstanding EU joint debt, including €68 billion in green bonds, and signals ongoing institutional resistance to fiscal integration despite the precedent established by pandemic-era joint issuance programmes.

4. ECB Analysis Identifies Investment Funds as Primary 'Bond Vigilantes'

Date: 14-08-2025 | Country: Eurozone | Categories: Market Analysis, Risk Assessment
The European Central Bank published comprehensive research on 14 August 2025 identifying investment funds as the primary 'bond vigilantes' in sovereign debt markets, holding up to 25% of outstanding government debt in vulnerable eurozone countries. The analysis revealed that during stress episodes, investment funds account for most net sales of sovereign bonds, whilst domestic households and insurance companies act as stabilising forces by purchasing bonds that investment funds divest. Countries identified as most vulnerable to this dynamic include Ireland, Greece, Spain, Italy, Cyprus, Portugal, Slovenia, and Slovakia. Non-eurozone investment funds demonstrated higher sensitivity to sovereign stress compared to domestic counterparts, creating potential fragmentation risks. This research provides critical insight into market microstructure during sovereign debt crises and informs the ECB's Transmission Protection Instrument framework for addressing unwarranted market fragmentation.
