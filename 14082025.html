Weekly Summary

During the week of 8–14 August 2025 the sovereign‑debt landscape was dominated by rating actions, restructuring milestones and debates about equitable burdens. S&P’s upgrade of India to BBB and Moody’s upgrade of Pakistan to Caa1 underscored confidence in fiscal consolidation and macro‑economic resilience. Sri Lanka reported that over 90 percent of its public external debt restructuring was implemented, while Ghana faced renewed calls for fiscal discipline and completion of debt negotiations. Zambia’s central bank held its policy rate amid inflation and restructuring delays. Malawi’s debt distress intensified, with large arrears to commercial creditors and hidden liabilities in the energy sector. Debt Justice research highlighted that private creditors receive the largest share of low‑income countries’ payments, fuelling calls for legal reforms. Articles also scrutinised the inadequacy of current macro‑fiscal frameworks for climate‑resilient investments and advocated for a Borrowers’ Club. Across regions, investors remained cautious, as reflected in Ukraine’s distressed bond pricing, while Angola considered a cautious return to Eurobond markets.
